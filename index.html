Brain regions are areas of the brain that perform specific functions.
These functions can be motor, sensory, cognitive, or emotional.

L Aud (Left Auditory Lobe) and R Aud (Right Auditory Lobe)

  • Location: Temporal lobe, in the primary auditory area
  • Function: Processing of auditory stimuli, including sounds, music, and language
  • Details:
    • The left auditory lobe is responsible for processing sounds in the left ear, while the right auditory lobe is responsible for processing sounds in the right ear.
    • It also deals with basic information about sounds, such as frequency, intensity, and duration.
    • Other areas of the auditory lobe are involved in processing more complex information, such as speech recognition, language comprehension, and sound localization in space.

Striate (Primary Visual Cortex)

  • Location: Occipital lobe, in the primary visual area
  • Function: Processing of visual stimuli, including shapes, colors, and movements
  • Details:
    • The primary visual cortex is responsible for processing basic information about images, such as brightness, contrast, and edges.
    • Other areas of the occipital lobe are involved in processing more complex information, such as object recognition, image understanding, and object localization in space.

L DMN (Left Default Mode Network) and R DMN (Right Default Mode Network)

  • Location: Frontal, parietal, and temporal lobes
  • Function: Memory, attention, and consciousness
  • Details:
    • The default mode network is a network of neurons that is active when we are not focused on a specific task.
    • The default mode network is involved in a variety of functions, including memory, attention, consciousness, and self-awareness.

Occ post (Secondary Visual Area)

  • Location: Occipital lobe, in the secondary visual area
  • Function: Processing of complex visual stimuli, including faces, objects, and scenes
  • Details:
    • The secondary visual area is responsible for processing more complex information about images, such as face recognition, object recognition, and scene recognition.
    • The secondary visual area is also involved in processing information about motion, depth, and color.

Motor (Primary Motor Cortex)

  • Location: Frontal lobe, in the primary motor cortex
  • Function: Control of voluntary movements
  • Details:
    • The primary motor cortex is responsible for controlling voluntary movements on the opposite side of the body.
    • The primary motor cortex is organized into a somatosensory map, where each region of the body is represented by a specific area of the cortex.

R DLPFC (Right Dorsolateral Prefrontal Cortex)

  • Location: Frontal lobe
  • Function: Cognitive control, planning, and task execution
  • Details:
    • The right dorsolateral prefrontal cortex is responsible for a variety of cognitive functions, including executive control, planning, and task execution.
    • The right dorsolateral prefrontal cortex is also involved in decision-making, problem-solving, and attention.

Basal (Basal Neuron Systems)

  • Location: Central nervous system, in the brainstem and diencephalon
  • Function: Motor control, emotion, and learning
  • Details:
    • The basal neuron systems are a set of structures involved in motor control, emotion, and learning.
    • They consist of two groups of neurons: the basal ganglia and the thalamus.
    • The basal ganglia are involved in motor control, emotion, and learning.
    • The thalamus is involved in transmitting sensory and motor information to the cerebral cortex.
